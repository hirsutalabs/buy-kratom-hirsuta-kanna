Borneo's Unique Growing Conditions

The Kalimantan region of Borneo sits directly on the equator, receiving 3,000–4,000mm of rainfall annually. The combination of deep alluvial soils enriched by the Kapuas River system and consistent tropical humidity creates growing conditions that produce Mitragyna speciosa with a particularly broad secondary alkaloid profile — speciociliatine, paynantheine and speciogynine alongside primary mitragynine. Trees are shade-grown to peak maturity before harvest.

Green Vein: The Balanced Middle

Within the Mitragyna speciosa vein-colour classification system, green vein represents the intermediate harvest stage. Leaves are collected when the central vein retains its green pigmentation — indicating a mitragynine-dominant alkaloid ratio with a broader supporting cast of secondary compounds than either white or red vein processing achieves. This makes green vein Borneo the preferred reference specimen for comparative vein-type studies.

Size Guide & Storage

250g starter, 500g standard, 1kg researcher, 5kg bulk. Store sealed in amber glass or food-grade HDPE container. Keep below 25°C, away from UV light and moisture. Do not refrigerate — condensation degrades powder quality. Shelf life 18–24 months from production date under correct conditions.

FAQ

What is the mitragynine content of Green Borneo Kratom?

HirsutaLab does not provide specific alkaloid percentages, as these vary naturally between batches based on harvest timing and leaf maturity. What distinguishes Green Borneo is the consistency of its broad secondary alkaloid profile — speciociliatine, paynantheine and speciogynine — which makes it a reliable reference specimen for comparative botanical research.

How does Green Borneo differ from Green Malay or Green Thai?

Geographic origin significantly shapes the alkaloid expression of Mitragyna speciosa. Borneo's deep alluvial soils and high annual rainfall produce a phenotype with notably broader secondary alkaloid diversity compared to Thai plateau varieties. Green Malay tends toward narrower alkaloid range; Green Borneo is the fuller-spectrum option for comparative studies.

Is this the same as Green Kalimantan Kratom?

Functionally identical. Kalimantan is the Indonesian name for the southern portion of Borneo where most commercial Mitragyna speciosa cultivation occurs. Green Borneo and Green Kalimantan refer to the same regional material — the difference is geographic naming convention, not botanical variety.

Can I order Green Borneo alongside other vein types for comparison?
