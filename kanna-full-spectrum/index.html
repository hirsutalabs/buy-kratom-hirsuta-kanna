The Full Spectrum Philosophy

The full spectrum concept is rooted in a principle recognised in botanical pharmacology: the combined effect of all alkaloids working in their natural ratios can produce a qualitatively different research profile than any single isolated compound. Minor alkaloids and co-factors that would be discarded in an isolate or concentration process can play significant supporting roles in the overall phytochemical character of the extract. Full Spectrum Kanna honours this natural complexity.

How Full Spectrum Differs from Other Extracts

Strong and Ultra Strong extracts maximise alkaloid density by removing secondary plant material. Crystal isolates specific alkaloids entirely. Happy Calming selectively emphasises the calming fraction. Full Spectrum does none of these — instead, it extracts the broadest possible alkaloid range while maintaining the natural inter-alkaloid ratios, providing a substrate that authentically represents the phytochemical composition of Sceletium tortuosum.

FAQ

Why choose Full Spectrum over a more concentrated extract?

Concentrated extracts maximise the quantity of primary alkaloids per gram. Full Spectrum preserves the original inter-alkaloid ratios, providing a research substrate that reflects the actual phytochemical composition of the plant. For whole-plant botanical study — where alkaloid ratios matter as much as absolute concentration — this authenticity is analytically significant.

Does Full Spectrum have lower alkaloid content than Strong Extract?

Per gram, yes. Full Spectrum is not designed to be the most potent — it is designed to be the most botanically authentic. The total alkaloid content per gram is lower than Strong or Ultra Strong, but the alkaloid diversity and ratio authenticity are higher.

Full Spectrum vs Happy Calming — what is the difference?

Full Spectrum preserves the complete natural profile without any selective modification. Happy Calming is processed to selectively emphasise the calming alkaloid fraction of Sceletium tortuosum. Full Spectrum is the more complete botanical option; Happy Calming is optimised for a specific expression.

Can I use Full Spectrum alongside Crystal for comparative research?

Yes — this is one of the most valuable uses of having multiple formats available. Comparing Full Spectrum (whole-plant ratio) against Crystal (isolated alkaloids) in the same study allows researchers to observe how alkaloid interactions differ from isolated compound behaviour.
