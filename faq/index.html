Frequently Asked Questions

Questions about Kratom, Kanna and Mitragyna Hirsuta research materials, alkaloid chemistry, EU regulations and ordering from HirsutaLab.

What is the legal status of Kratom (Mitragyna speciosa) in the EU?

Kratom legal status varies significantly between EU member states. As of 2025, Mitragyna speciosa is a controlled substance in several EU countries including Romania, Poland and Lithuania, while remaining legal for research and collection in Germany, Netherlands, France, Czech Republic, Hungary and others. What is the difference between mitragynine and 7-hydroxymitragynine?

Mitragynine (MW 398.5) is the dominant alkaloid produced by Mitragyna speciosa via primary biosynthesis. 7-Hydroxymitragynine (7-HMG, MW 414.5) is formed from mitragynine by enzymatic hydroxylation at the C7 position of the indole scaffold — a maturity-dependent oxidation that increases progressively from white vein (trace) through green vein to red vein (maximum). Green Borneo Kratom captures the mid-maturity snapshot with low 7-HMG and full minor alkaloid complement. Red Maeng Da Nano Powder represents full maturity with elevated 7-HMG.

What is speciociliatine and why does it matter for research?

Speciociliatine is the second most abundant alkaloid in Mitragyna speciosa by weight — a C3 epimer of mitragynine sharing the same molecular formula (C23H30N2O4) but differing in spatial configuration at carbon C3. This stereochemical difference produces a measurably distinct receptor binding profile in in vitro assays. Speciociliatine is best preserved in green-vein material harvested at mid-maturity; shade-drying below 40°C prevents thermal degradation. HirsutaLab Green Borneo Kratom is the reference material for complete speciociliatine retention.

How do I extract and isolate Kratom alkaloids for HPLC analysis?

Standard protocol: (1) Extract 0.5g powder in 10ml 80% ethanol, sonicate 30 min, centrifuge. (2) For cleanup: acid-base partition — dissolve residue in 0.1M HCl (pH 3), wash with DCM to remove non-basic compounds, basify to pH 9-10 with ammonia, extract free bases with DCM. (3) For SPE: MCX sorbent, load at pH 5, wash with water and 5% methanol, elute with 5% ammonia in methanol. (4) HPLC: C18 column, gradient 30-70% acetonitrile in 0.1% formic acid, 15 min, UV 254nm. Elution order: speciociliatine (~6 min), mitragynine (~8 min), speciogynine (~9 min), paynantheine (~11 min).

What is Kanna (Sceletium tortuosum) and is it legal in the EU?

Sceletium tortuosum — Kanna or Kougoed — is a South African succulent with a documented ethnobotanical history exceeding four centuries. It produces mesembrine-type benzofuranone alkaloids (mesembrine, mesembrenone, mesembrenol, mesembranol) derived from the phenylethylamine biosynthetic pathway — structurally unrelated to Kratom alkaloids. Kanna is legal for purchase and research in all EU member states as of 2025 with no controlled substance scheduling. HirsutaLab sells Kanna exclusively as a research and collection material.

What is the difference between Kanna Full Spectrum, Strong and Crystal Ultimate?

They sit at different points on a concentration-purity axis. Full Spectrum (~5:1): low-ethanol ambient-temperature extraction preserves all four mesembrine alkaloids in natural proportions — the natural-ratio baseline. Strong (10:1): standard ethanol extraction concentrates alkaloids 10x, preferring mesembrine and mesembrenone. Ultra Strong (20:1): double extraction reaches maximum powder density. Crystal Ultimate: acid-base crystallisation isolate — matrix removed, crystalline alkaloid, highest purity for HPLC calibration and NMR structural work.

How should I store botanical research materials?

All HirsutaLab materials: sealed container at room temperature (15-25°C), away from light, heat and humidity. Do not refrigerate — condensation on opening degrades powders. Shelf life sealed: Kratom and Mitragyna Hirsuta leaf powders 18-24 months; Kanna extracts 18 months (hygroscopic, reseal immediately after use); Crystal Ultimate alkaloid isolate 24 months in amber glass with desiccant. Signs of degradation: colour darkening, musty odour, clumping.

What is the difference between Mitragyna speciosa and Mitragyna hirsuta?

Both belong to genus Mitragyna, family Rubiaceae, but produce chemically distinct alkaloid classes. Mitragyna speciosa: indole alkaloids (tryptamine biosynthesis) — mitragynine, 7-HMG, speciociliatine. Mitragyna hirsuta: oxindole alkaloids (mitraphylline, isomitraphylline) — no mitragynine. This chemical divergence within a closely related genus makes hirsuta invaluable for comparative alkaloid biosynthesis research and as a negative reference control in mitragynine immunoassay development.
