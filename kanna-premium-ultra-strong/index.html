Double Extraction Process: Technical Detail

The Ultra Strong production starts from Strong (10:1) extract. This is re-dissolved in dilute hydrochloric or acetic acid (pH 3–4), where mesembrine-type alkaloids form water-soluble acid-addition salts. The solution is filtered through activated charcoal to remove plant pigments (primarily chlorophyll degradation products). The clarified acid solution is then slowly basified to pH 9–10 using ammonia or sodium hydroxide, causing the free-base alkaloids to become insoluble and precipitate. The precipitate is filtered, washed with cold water to remove residual salts, and dried at 40°C. The result is a powder with substantially higher alkaloid concentration per gram than the Strong extract.

Alkaloid Distribution at 20:1 Concentration

The double-extraction process preferentially concentrates alkaloids with higher lipophilicity and crystallisation tendency: mesembrine and mesembrenone dominate in Ultra Strong due to their greater affinity for the base-precipitation step. Mesembrenol and mesembranol — more hydrophilic — are partially retained in the aqueous wash phase and are present in reduced proportion compared to Strong or Full Spectrum extracts. Researchers requiring the complete four-alkaloid natural ratio should choose Full Spectrum; Ultra Strong is optimal when maximum mesembrine/mesembrenone density per gram is the priority.

Research Applications: HPLC Calibration and High-Density Reference

Ultra Strong is particularly suited for: HPLC calibration curve preparation where a high-concentration standard reduces the volume required; extraction yield studies where a dense input material minimises weighing error; comparative pharmacological screening requiring maximum alkaloid density per gram; and as an intermediate step toward Crystal Ultimate crystalline isolate production (Ultra Strong is the starting material for acid-base crystallisation).

Frequently Asked Questions

How does Ultra Strong differ from Crystal Ultimate?

Ultra Strong (~20:1) is a concentrated plant extract with residual plant-matrix material remaining despite two extraction cycles. Crystal Ultimate undergoes an additional acid-base crystallisation step that removes virtually all remaining matrix components, yielding a crystalline alkaloid isolate of significantly greater purity. Ultra Strong: higher density than Strong, some matrix residue. Crystal Ultimate: maximum purity, minimal matrix.

What mesembrine percentage per gram can I expect?

Processing guideline 20:1. Raw plant: 0.3–1.0% mesembrine-type total. At 20:1: approximately 6–20% per gram depending on starting material quality. This is a guideline range, not a certified specification — batch HPLC analysis recommended for precise quantification.

Can Ultra Strong be purified to Crystal Ultimate quality in-house?

Yes, in principle. Ultra Strong is the intermediate material from which Crystal Ultimate is produced. In-lab further purification requires acid-base crystallisation: dissolve in dilute acid (pH 3), filter through activated charcoal, basify slowly to pH 9–10, filter crystals, wash with cold water, dry at 40°C. Standard alkaloid isolation protocol.

Why does Ultra Strong have a different alkaloid ratio than Full Spectrum?

Double extraction preferentially concentrates lipophilic alkaloids (mesembrine, mesembrenone) due to their higher affinity for the acid-base precipitation process. The more hydrophilic alkaloids (mesembrenol, mesembranol) are partially lost to the aqueous wash phase in each cycle. This selective enrichment is a natural consequence of differential solubility — not an intentional purification step.
