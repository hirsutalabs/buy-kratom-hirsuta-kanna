Mitragyna hirsuta, known locally in Thailand as Kra Thum Khok, is a tropical tree native to the lowland forests of Southeast Asia. A close botanical cousin of Mitragyna speciosa (Kratom), it belongs to the same Rubiaceae family but carries a distinctly different alkaloid fingerprint dominated by oxindoles — primarily mitraphylline and isomitraphylline — rather than the indole alkaloids found in Kratom.

The oxindole alkaloid profile — particularly mitraphylline — has drawn increasing academic attention in recent years, making this an ideal specimen for phytochemical reference collections.

FAQ

Is Mitragyna Hirsuta the same as Kratom?

No. Both belong to the genus Mitragyna and the Rubiaceae family, but they are distinct species. Mitragyna speciosa (Kratom) contains primarily indole alkaloids (mitragynine, 7-hydroxymitragynine), while Mitragyna hirsuta contains mainly oxindole alkaloids (mitraphylline, isomitraphylline). Their botanical profiles and legal statuses differ significantly across jurisdictions.
