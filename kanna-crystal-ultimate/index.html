The Crystallisation Process

Crystal formation requires a fundamentally different processing pathway from conventional powder extracts. Following initial extraction, the alkaloid concentrate undergoes a solvent-mediated crystallisation step that selectively nucleates specific alkaloid compounds into defined solid crystal structures. The crystals are then collected, washed to remove residual solvent and processed material, and dried under precisely controlled conditions. The result is a product of exceptional visual clarity and chemical purity.

Analytical Value of Crystalline Extracts

Crystalline botanical extracts offer distinct advantages in research contexts. Unlike amorphous powders where alkaloid distribution can be uneven, crystal extracts have a defined molecular structure. This allows more precise weighing and quantification per unit, reduces handling variation, and provides a chemically consistent reference standard. For ethnobotanical collectors building high-precision libraries, Crystal is the gold standard.

FAQ

What makes Crystal different from all other Kanna extract formats?

Crystal Kanna undergoes an additional crystallisation step beyond standard extraction, organising specific alkaloids into defined solid crystal structures. Unlike amorphous powders, crystals have uniform molecular distribution, higher per-gram alkaloid content, and greater analytical precision — making them uniquely valuable for precision botanical research.

Which alkaloids are concentrated in Kanna Crystal?

The crystallisation process selectively concentrates the primary mesembrine-group alkaloids of Sceletium tortuosum. For detailed compositional information, contact HirsutaLab directly through their website.

Crystal vs Full Spectrum — which should I choose?

Full Spectrum preserves the complete natural alkaloid matrix of the plant including all minor compounds. Crystal is the opposite approach: maximum concentration of primary alkaloids through selective isolation. Choose Full Spectrum for whole-plant botanical authenticity; choose Crystal for precision alkaloid analysis and high-potency reference standards.

How should Crystal Kanna be stored?

Crystal extracts are sensitive to moisture, which can cause reversion from crystalline to amorphous form. Store in a tightly sealed amber glass container with a silica gel desiccant, in a consistently cool, dark location. Avoid any temperature fluctuations. Properly stored crystal retains form and potency for 24+ months.
